Bravida Case Study

How might we improve service experience for Bravida´s customers?

Responsibilites

I conducted user interviews to understand customer´s behaviors, created artifacts for presenting insights to the client. In the second phase of this project, I created wireframes and prototypes and tested those with users.


About Bravida

Bravida works with all systems that bring buildings to life. Bravida offers service and installation across several areas of technology: electrical, heating & plumbing, ventilation and much more. Bravida has historically been a traditional company in a conservative industry. However, making efficient use of possibilities of the technology of today is a top priority that has accelerated the digitalization journey within Bravida.

Problem Statement

Bravida came to Hyperisland asking for help to get insights into their customer's behaviors to find solutions for improving the service experience for their customers.

Solution

Web portal 2.0

Time Frame

2 months

Tools Used
  • Miro
  • Figma
  • Adobe Photoshop
  • Adobe Premiere Pro

UX Process

In the beginning, we aligned on the following goals with our client to decide the success of this project:

  • Get insights into users behaviour, needs and their preferences.
  • Finding opportunity areas (key areas) based on user research
  • Creating new design solution for improving customer ordering experience

Every UX process is different depending upon the task at hand. We took the double diamond approach for this project. In the first phase, we discover about the Bravida customer´s behaviors.


User Research

In order to understand our users, me and my team conducted 5 user interviews.We asked open questions to them about their current ordering experience and their experience with Bravida.Also talked about their expectations when it comes to placing an order to get service for a facility under their supervision.

We used miro to gather and analyze data.As a team we were able to find patterns in the data by putting all data on the miro board.

We performed expert review analysis to identify usability problems on the existing portal. We put ourselves in users' shoes and tried to accomplish tasks like ordering service on the web portal, reviewing the order, and tracking the order status.

We found some usability issues regarding unclear navigation on the portal, not knowing where to click. As a user, I found it hard to understand the technical language on the portal for both placing and viewing the status of the order. For adding an order , the user has to wait for almost 1 minute for the system to respond

By exploring the web portal ourselves, we have been able to understand the problem more accurately.

User Research Insights

Persona

Based on the collected data from user interviews, we created a fictitious persona Loretta Lindqvist to understand the user's needs, experiences, behaviors, and goals. It helped us to identify the users for whom we needed to design.

By understanding our users we got insights that users of Bravida are people who work on the managerial level and their daily work life includes multitasking. They are responsible for managing multiple facilities on multiple sites. They needed to be sure that everything works flawlessly under their supervision. Since their hands are already full of many tasks, they wish for an easy and transparent journey while placing an order on the Bravida system. The current design of the web portal failed to provide this experience to their customers which made their customers dependent on people.

As we all know in the global digitization era people dependent culture is not sustainable in the long run. Especially when Bravida has 50,000 customers and even adding more with time.

Storyboard

In the storyboard, we lay down an existing user journey for ordering service with Bravida. This storyboard was also helpful for us to present user journeys to the client requirements for the product.

Customer Journey

In the envisioned journey we focus on the pain points which we were ambitious to solve with new design solutions.

With our new design, we proposed features for the users to view historical order data of the site. That could help users to make better decisions while placing a service order. We aimed to create an easy service order form. We proposed to use user-friendly language on the web portal. The navigation should feel smooth .Features to get real time updates to track an order on the web portal.

Value Proposition Chart

Based on the insights gathered from user research we moved on to our second goal to find opportunities to improve customer experience. We mapped opportunities with business needs in the value proposition chart. In this chart, I listed the gain creators, pain relievers, and products and services. We further ranked each point from nice to have -to essential in terms of value to the customer.

Design Process

At the beginning of the development phase, we again took a divergent approach to come up with all possible solutions based on our chosen opportunity area. We chose to improve the ordering experience for the user to give them an overall better service experience.

Hypotheses:

  • "We believed use of non technical language could improve the overall experience of users on the web portal."
  • "We believed categorising facilities according to service type could help users to navigate through sites on web portals."
  • "We believe if we improve the visual hierarchy on the system it will help the users to use the system to its full potential."
  • "We believe that implementing search and filter options in the web portal, along with customizing features, will simplify the user’s need to navigate through information on the web portal."

Lofi Prototypes

We tested our first iteration with the Bravida business development team who works closely with developers for the next release of the web portal.By testing the prototype our hypothesis no 2 turns out false due to digital infrastructure design of Bravida.Every site for particular service gets registered separately as a separate entity in the Bravida database.


Testing Insights

By testing the 2nd iteration with two users we discovered:

  • Order type & error type, reference no. wasn´t the need of the customer.
  • There was a use case where the user need to edit the address of the site
  • Having filter options on the top of the table was something they were not used to.
  • Customizing features was not really a requirement for the user since the user wanted to spend very little time on the system.
  • History or active navigation was not visible enough. Maybe it will take some time for users to learn the system.

Final Solution

We updated our prototypes based on the insights we had from user testing. For our final design, we followed Bravida´s brand guidelines.

Takeaways

Since we could test only with two users we couldn´t really verify or falsify our assumptions. Also in bravida there are different clients who take different kinds of services from users,So in the best of world, we could have tested this with different kinds of users.
