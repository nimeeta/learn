Ericsson Case Study

How might we create a design solution for the target user to search or traverse through the organization hierarchy easily and quickly?


About Ericsson

Ericsson is one of the leading providers of Information and Communication Technology (ICT) to service providers.They enable the full value of connectivity by creating game-changing technology and services that are easy to use, adopt, and scale, making their customers successful in a fully connected world.

Problem Statement

It was a collaboration between HyperIsland and Ericsson. Our client gave us a brief to design an interactive prototype for visualizing the tree hierarchy of an organization. Also, the prototype should allow the user to perform some call to action at node levels. The solution needs to be generic in use.

Solution

Interactive prototype to visualize tree hierarchy of nodes /devices and to perform call to actions on node level.

Responsibilites

I created use-case flows to understand the overall business use case related to handling a large organizational hierarchy of nodes/massive devices. Involved in wireframing, prototyping in Adobe XD.

Time Frame

1 month

Tools Used
  • Miro
  • Adobe XD
  • Adobe Photoshop
  • Adobe Premiere Pro

UX Process

It was a Rapid prototyping project so we used design thinking process to get the task in hand done. Our client briefed us about the current situation, the pain points of the users, their needs, and the problems they faced with an existing system. It helped us to empathize with the users and understand their desires.

Persona

So our user was a corporate account manager who is a dedicated account manager for medium-large enterprises.He is a single point of contact and relationship owner for a defined set of customers.Our user needs to use multiple tools in daily work.So basically he needs system in which he can quickly checks the organization hierarchy and can take necessary actions for group of people or single people.

Ideation-Solutions Brainstorm

We started the ideation process by defining the scope and constraints of the project to align within the team and keep track of execution priority. And to understand the limitation under which we are supposed to work.

Scope

  • Our scope was to create a visual design for the target user to search or traverse through the distributed tree easily and quickly.
  • Users should be able to perform some node-specific actions on each level.
  • Need to follow Ericsson BSS UX recommendation and brands guidelines

Constraint

  • Design should solve multiple use cases.

Priority List

We made a priority list mentioning the crucial features according to their importance. So we know what we need to implement first to achieve our goal. So our users mainly needed easy navigation, zooming hierarchy functionality, a clear overview of top-level hierarchy, and clear data visualization when users look for node-level data. Our second priority was to implement search and filtering features at all node levels, different view options to view data, status visibility at the node level. The third priority was to implement a map view. The fourth priority was to implement notification or implementation of a feedback system for the user to clearly understand where is in his current visualization of hierarchy at all times.

Wireframes & Lofi prototype

We started with LoFi prototype workshops together using existing design guidelines and we were also in touch with clients who updated us from time to time with information as and when they had more details from the research.

Our initial wireframes were basically to discuss ideas together. We picked the best in everyone's design ideas and created the Lofi prototype. So we started with the idea of displaying node information on the cards and designing the left navigation as of the left panel of windows. Apart from the card design, we decided to give users a map view of the hierarchy to navigate.


Usability Testing

In our 3rd week we conducted usability testing with users from Ericsson and we got feedback from them. They would like to have some call to action for groups of leaves at last level .

Another insight from user testing was user would like to view high level information of whole organization hierarchy from the landing page without navigating through the pages.So we come with the idea of having and relation graph view as another way to view the hierarchy along with map view.
